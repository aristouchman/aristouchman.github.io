Why Control and Audit Are the Future of Banking

By Felix Uchenna Okoh | April 18, 2025

There was a time I believed banking was all about meeting sales targets, acquiring customers, and maintaining relationships. But the deeper I went, the more I realized the real backbone of sustainable banking isn't just sales—it's control.

I recall a fund transfer incident that reshaped my entire outlook on the banking profession. It was late 2021, during a relatively quiet morning. A customer’s representative walked in with a duly signed instruction to transfer ₦3.2 million to a third-party account. On paper, everything looked fine. The mandate bore the correct signature, and the rep had previously handled transactions on behalf of the customer.

However, something didn't feel right. The beneficiary was unfamiliar — neither listed in previous mandates nor part of the customer's routine transfer pattern. I requested to put a confirmation call through to the account owner.

To my surprise, the customer was alarmed. He confirmed he did not authorize any such transfer and hadn't sent his rep to the branch that day. We immediately flagged the transaction. Internal control was looped in, and the transaction was reversed before it could leave the bank.

Further investigation revealed that the rep had forged the mandate, using an older copy for selfish gain. The customer was grateful, but that day stuck with me. If we had processed the instruction without scrutiny, the funds would have been lost—possibly unrecoverable.

That experience deepened my respect for internal control and the power of due diligence. It wasn’t just about processing transactions, it was about protecting customers—even from those closest to them.

In my current role as a Relationship Manager, I no longer see Control and Audit as back-end units. I see them as strategic partners. I now engage them on documentation reviews, exception reporting, and even periodic KYC profiling. The aim is simple: build a system that not only moves money but protects integrity.

Technology is making transactions faster—but also more vulnerable. As the threat of social engineering, identity fraud, and insider abuse grows, the most important asset in banking becomes trust. And trust is built on **strong controls**.

This is why I’m transitioning deeper into Audit, Compliance, and Risk. I’ve started upskilling with certifications on financial crime, data ethics, and cyber security. I’ve even begun applying basic Python for reconciling internal datasets. Every step I take in this direction strengthens my commitment to secure, ethical banking.

The future of banking isn’t just about innovation. It’s about fortifying trust, one verified transaction at a time. Control is no longer optional—it’s the new competitive edge.
